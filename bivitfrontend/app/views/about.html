About Phrase

Our Story Phrase was created in 2010 by Mel Baiada as a summer project for high school students. Since then, teams of high school students have signed on every year to continue work on the project. Phrase is a social annotation platform for K-12 classrooms. Phrase allows students to annotate essays and articles with teacher supervision. Teachers can comment on their students' annotations. We are located in the offices of Basecamp Ventures, in Moorestown, New Jersey. Phrase will be ready for beta testing in the 2015-2016 school year. If you're interested in beta testing Phrase in your classroom, or have any further questions about Phrase, please visit our Contact Us page.

Assets and Guidelines

Colors

Phrase's website has a variety of colors, but one main color scheme is implemented. Phrase's design team used Google Material Design's color palettes as a reference for the team's color choices. Phrase's color scheme includes a primary/base palette and a secondary/accent palette. Indigo (500 or #3F51B5) is used for the primary/base color and light blue (A200 or #40C4FF) is the secondary/accent color.



































The colors used to display text include black (#000000), white (#FFFFFF), grey (400 or #BDBDBD), and grey (600 or #757575).

























The site's background colors include white (#FFFFFF) and grey (200 or #EEEEEE).

Logo Icon

This is the Phrase single icon. The icon's original size is 696px by 1302px with a resolution of 300 px/in.















The icon has very specific colors. The outmost part of the pencil's tip is black (#000000) and the inmost part of the pencil's tip is Amber (100 or #FFECB3). The body of the pencil is divided into three sections. We used three colors from the Amber color palette that transition from dark to light. From left to right, the colors are: Amber (800 or #FF8F00), Amber (600 or #FFB300), and Amber (300 or #FFD54F). The metal section of the pencil has a large rectangle that is grey (#B7B7B7) and two smaller horizontal rectangles that are grey (#555555). The pencil's eraser is red (300 or #E57373).
